Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our online Quran classes, enrollment process, and learning methods

How do I start learning Quran online with iHadi?

Starting your online Quran learning journey is simple! First, book a free trial class through our website. During the trial, our qualified teacher will assess your current level and recommend the best course for you. After the trial, you can choose your preferred plan (2, 3, or 5 days per week) and schedule that fits your routine. We'll then assign you a dedicated teacher and provide all necessary learning materials.

What qualifications do your Quran teachers have?

All our teachers are certified Quran instructors with extensive experience in online teaching. They have completed formal Islamic education, hold Ijazah (certification) in Quranic recitation and Tajweed, and many are Huffaz (memorizers of the entire Quran). We have both male and female teachers available to accommodate student preferences and ensure a comfortable learning environment.

Is online Quran learning effective for children?

Absolutely! Our online Quran classes for kids are specially designed to be engaging and interactive. We use child-friendly teaching methods, colorful learning materials, and fun activities to keep children interested. Our teachers are experienced in working with children and understand how to make learning enjoyable while maintaining Islamic values and respect for the Quran.

What technology do I need for online Quran classes?

You need a stable internet connection, a computer/laptop/tablet with a webcam and microphone, or a smartphone. We use popular video conferencing platforms like Zoom, Skype, or WhatsApp video calls. We'll provide detailed setup instructions and technical support to ensure smooth classes. No special software installation is required.

Can I change my class schedule if needed?

Yes, we offer flexible scheduling to accommodate your needs. You can discuss your preferred timing with your teacher and make adjustments when necessary. We understand that life circumstances can change, so we work with you to find mutually convenient times. We also offer makeup classes if you miss a session due to unavoidable circumstances.

How long does it take to complete a Quran memorization course?

The duration depends on several factors including your current level, memorization goals, class frequency, and daily practice time. Complete Quran memorization (Hifz) typically takes 2-4 years with regular study. However, we also offer partial memorization programs for specific Surahs or sections. Your teacher will create a personalized plan based on your goals and provide regular progress assessments.

Do you offer classes for complete beginners?

Yes! We welcome students of all levels, including complete beginners. Our Basic Quran Reading course is perfect for those who cannot read Arabic at all. We start with Arabic alphabet recognition, letter sounds, and gradually progress to reading simple words and verses. Our teachers are patient and experienced in working with beginners, ensuring a comfortable learning pace.

What is included in the free trial class?

The free trial class includes a 30-minute session with one of our qualified teachers. During this time, we'll assess your current Quran reading level, discuss your learning goals, demonstrate our teaching methodology, and answer any questions you may have. This helps you experience our teaching quality before making a commitment. No payment is required for the trial class.

Are there separate classes for men and women?

Yes, we respect Islamic guidelines and offer both male and female teachers. Female students and young children can choose female teachers, while male students can select male teachers. We ensure that all our teaching arrangements align with Islamic values and provide a comfortable learning environment for everyone.

How do you track student progress?

We maintain detailed progress records for each student, including lessons completed, areas of improvement, and achievements. Teachers provide regular feedback to students and parents, conduct periodic assessments, and issue certificates upon course completion. We also provide monthly progress reports to help students track their Quranic learning journey.
